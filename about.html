About Me

Hello! I'm Amir Saman Mirjalili, a passionate researcher and engineer specializing in robotics and mechatronics. I recently completed my M.Sc. in Mechatronics Engineering from K. N. Toosi University of Technology in Tehran, Iran. Currently, I'm an active researcher at the Advanced Robotics and Automated Systems (ARAS) Lab, where I continue to explore and contribute to cutting-edge robotics research.

With a strong background in mechanical engineering and a keen interest in cutting-edge technologies, I strive to contribute to the advancement of robotics and its applications in various fields. I am deeply passionate about pursuing a PhD to further my research and make significant contributions to the field of robotics, particularly in areas such as parallel robots, kinematic calibration, and advanced control systems.

Research Interests

Robotics:

  • Modeling and Designing Robots
  • Localization & Pose Estimation
  • Factor Graphs & Optimization
  • Design & Embedded Hardware
  • Optimal Control & Model Predictive Control (MPC)
  • 3D CAD Design

Artificial Intelligence:

  • Machine Learning: Supervised & Unsupervised Learning, Physics Informed Machine Learning
  • Reinforcement Learning: Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient (DDPG)
  • Computer Vision: Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), YOLO, Pose Detection, Transfer Learning

Machine Vision:

  • 3D Reconstruction: Triangulation, Stereo-vision, Bundle Adjustment
  • Visual Odometry (VO), Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM)

I'm eager to pursue doctoral studies that will allow me to delve deeper into these research areas and contribute to groundbreaking advancements in robotics. My goal is to develop innovative solutions that bridge the gap between theoretical robotics and practical applications, potentially revolutionizing fields such as medical robotics, industrial automation, and space exploration.

I'm always eager to collaborate on exciting projects and discuss new ideas in the field of robotics and AI. Feel free to reach out if you'd like to connect, discuss potential PhD opportunities, or learn more about my work!
